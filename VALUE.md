# Why OpenTrial Matters

## The Problem
Most clinical trials are designed using rule-of-thumb sample-size calculations and frequentist endpoints. The result: trials are routinely over-powered (wasting patient exposure and money) or under-powered (failing for the wrong reason and burying potentially good drugs). Bayesian designs — which formally incorporate precedent, adaptive interim looks, and prior evidence — produce smaller, faster, more ethical trials, but they are mathematically and computationally demanding to set up.

## What OpenTrial Does
Automates the design step. A user describes the trial; the engine pulls precedent from prior trials, literature, safety signals, and target biology, then returns a Bayesian sample-size calculation with operating characteristics and full citation provenance. The dossier is reproducible and reviewer-ready.

## Who Benefits
- Investigator-initiated trial groups
- Statistician-light pharma teams
- Regulatory consultants preparing pre-IND packages
- Oncology cooperative groups designing platform trials

## Why Now
ClinicalTrials.gov's structured AACT data, the maturation of probabilistic-programming libraries (PyMC, Stan), and LLMs that can summarize trial precedent make the previously manual literature-grounding step automatable for the first time.

## The Bigger Picture
Better-designed trials mean fewer patients exposed to ineffective therapies and faster decisions on which drugs to abandon. The R&D-productivity leverage from improving even 10% of trial designs is enormous — and the patients enrolled in those trials are the most direct beneficiaries.
